About the Running Plan Generator

The running plan generator is a tool designed to help runners of all levels achieve their desired running distance and fitness goals. Whether you're just starting out or looking to take your running to the next level, the generator can provide you with a customized plan that takes into account your current level of fitness and the amount of time you have available to train.

When using the generator, you'll first be prompted to enter your current running distance in either miles or kilometers. This information helps the generator determine your starting point and create a plan that is challenging yet achievable. Next, you'll be asked to enter the desired distance you want to reach, along with the number of weeks you have to train. This information allows the generator to create a training plan that takes into account the amount of time you have available to reach your goal.

In addition to these basic details, the running plan generator also asks which days of the week you would prefer to run on. This information is used to create a schedule that fits around your existing commitments and allows you to prioritize your training according to your needs.

It's worth noting that the running plan generator is designed with an ideal timeframe of 4-24 weeks in mind. If you're looking to prepare for a race that falls within this time frame and covers a distance of 5 to 100 km, the generator should be able to provide you with a comprehensive and effective training plan. However, if you're looking to prepare for a race outside of this time frame or distance, the generator may not be as effective.

Overall, the running plan generator is a valuable tool for anyone looking to improve their running skills and reach their desired distance and fitness goals. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned runner, the generator can help you create a customized training plan that is tailored to your needs and schedule.

Types of Training Runs

Recovery Run

Easy runs that help you recover from harder workouts.

Race Pace

Runs done at your goal race pace to prepare for race day.

Fartlek

Speed play runs that mix intervals of fast running with slower recovery periods.

Hills

Runs that include uphill segments to build strength and endurance.

Progressive

Runs that start slow and finish fast, building speed gradually.

Interval

Runs that include repeated short bursts of high intensity running followed by rest or low intensity.

Rest

Days designated for complete rest to allow your body to recover.

Cross Training

Activities other than running that help improve fitness and prevent injury.

How to Use These Plans

Our training plans are designed to cater to all levels of runners. Here's how you can get the most out of them:

  • Choose your race distance and the plan that fits your current fitness level.
  • Use our Run Calculators to fine-tune your paces and training zones.
  • Follow the weekly schedule, including rest days to prevent overtraining.

Difference Between Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced Plans

Our plans are tailored to suit different levels of experience and goals:

  • Beginner: Focus on completing the race. Includes 3-4 days of training per week with essential mileage.
  • Intermediate: Mixes different types of workouts, including speed work and long runs, with 4-5 days of training per week.
  • Advanced: Includes goal or recent race paces, with 4-6 days of training per week, aiming to optimize performance.
