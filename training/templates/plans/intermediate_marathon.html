How It Works

This tool will generate an intermediate marathon training plan based on your selected number of weeks and preferred unit of measurement.

Types of Runs

Easy Runs: Comfortable pace, usually run at a conversational pace.

Medium Runs: Include sessions like Fartlek, Tempo, Progression, Long Intervals, and Threshold.

Speed Runs: Include 100m Intervals, 400m Intervals, 800m Intervals, and Hill Repeats.

Long Runs: Steady, longer-distance runs to build endurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about generating and using your intermediate marathon training plan.

How do I choose the right training distance?

Select the race distance you are preparing for. The intermediate marathon plan is designed specifically for the marathon distance.

How many weeks should I train?

We offer plans ranging from 16 to 22 weeks. Choose the duration that fits your schedule and training level. More weeks allow for a more gradual build-up and better adaptation.

What if I need to change the units?

You can choose between miles and kilometers in the "Unit" dropdown. The training plan will adjust accordingly.

What if I miss a week?

If you miss a week, don't try to make up all the missed workouts. Continue with your plan, and adjust the intensity if necessary. It's better to progress steadily than risk injury by overtraining.

Can I download or email my training plan?

Yes! After generating your plan, you can download it as a PDF or send it to your email using the provided buttons.

What if I'm looking for a more challenging plan?

If you're looking for more difficult training plans, check out our advanced marathon training plan. These plans include additional workouts and higher mileage to help you reach your goals.
