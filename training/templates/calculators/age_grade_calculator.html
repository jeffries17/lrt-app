Understanding Age-Grading in Running

As runners age or compete across different sexes and distances, performance comparisons become challenging. Our age-grading calculator provides a standardized way to compare these performances irrespective of these variables.

What is Age-Grading?

Age-grading measures and compares the quality of performances by adjusting for age, sex, and race distance. Through a sophisticated calculation, it produces a percentage that benchmarks any given performance against world records, allowing for fair comparisons across different categories.

This enables effective grading of your runs, where each distance has a unique age-graded score. It's an excellent tool for comparing performances of athletes who are not directly competitive due to age or gender differences, such as comparing a 50-year-old woman's marathon time with a 15-year-old boy's 5K time.

Practical Applications of Age-Grading

Age grading is not just for comparing athletes; it's also invaluable for personal benchmarking. Runners can compare their performances over the years, estimate past or future capabilities, or even determine their best racing distances. For instance, a lower age-grading in a half marathon compared to shorter races might suggest a need to improve endurance.

The higher the age-grading percentage, the closer the performance is to the world-class standard, with 100% representing a world record-equivalent performance, 90% representing world class, 80% national class, 70% regional class, and 60% local class.
